About Me

I am passionate about data and math.


I am an aspiring data scientist and math PhD student at The University of Texas at Austin. My thesis constructs (or discovers) a new type of Koszul duality that has applications to physics. Before graduate school, I worked on a variety of data driven projects for the Department of Defense.


In today's age of social media and unprecedented access to human behavior, data has the power to answer critical questions and deliver key, actionable insights. I am interested in pushing the boundaries of what we can learn with data.

My Projects

  • Geolocation of Unreliable UFO Sightings. geolocation-of-ufo


    While I do not believe in aliens myself, it's fascinating to me that so many people "see" ufo's in the sky. Furthermore, sightings in the U.S. continue to increase every year.


    In this project, I explore (extremely unreliable) ufo sighting data from NUFORC. Much of the work involved cleaning, reworking, and reorganizing this data. I develop a web application that allows a user to explore interactive maps and charts of ufo sighting data over time. Lastly, I analyze statistically significant abberations in the data and suggest possible causes for such abberations.
